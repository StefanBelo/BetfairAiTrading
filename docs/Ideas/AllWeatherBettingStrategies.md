## 🔍 Selection Criteria and Analysis

1. **Course & Surface Experience**
   - Horses that have previously performed well on a given AW track (Kempton, Wolverhampton, etc.) are privileged.
   - Be cautious with runners unproven on Tapeta surfaces; past form on different AW types doesn't always translate.

2. **Distance & Class Fit**
   - Check recent runs at the same distance and in similar class.
   - Consider step‑up or drop in class as a value indicator.

3. **Trainer/Jockey Combinations**
   - Certain connections (e.g., Saeed bin Suroor & Kevin Stott, Marco Botti, Paul Henderson) were highlighted for their AW strike rates. Follow horses from high‑percentage trainers and watch jockey claims.
   - Note when a jockey takes off weight or when trainers are in form (multiple winners recently).

4. **Race Fitness**
   - Preference for horses with recent runs unless a trainer has a reputation for firing them fresh (e.g., Quick returners like Keith Dalgleish’s horses).
   - Beware lay‑offs and unproven fitness—race‑fit animals often deliver value.

5. **Draw and Running Style**
   - A wide draw might be less concerning over longer trips; front‑running vs. held‑up horses matter depending on pace and track bias.

6. **Market Signals**
   - Prices drifting or shortening hint at underlying expectations (e.g., Godolphin entries attracting money suggest readiness).
   - Look for quotable value (e.g., outsider of two similar stablemates often overperforms).
